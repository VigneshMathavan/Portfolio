OVERVIEW

The CDP market is growing fast — and getting harder to differentiate in

CustomerLabs is a no-code Customer Data Platform (CDP) that helps marketers connect first-party data to ad platforms without relying on engineers. As cookie deprecation and iOS tracking changes force brands to rethink their data infrastructure, CDPs have moved from a nice-to-have to a core stack component.

But the CDP market has exploded — Segment (Twilio), mParticle, Rudderstack, Adobe Real-Time CDP, and a growing tier of newer players are all competing for the same marketing budget. This competitive analysis maps where CustomerLabs sits in this landscape and what positioning moves are available to it.

THE MARKET CONTEXT

"First-party data is now the most valuable asset a brand can own. Every CDP is selling that promise. CustomerLabs' edge is removing the engineering bottleneck — but the question is whether that's a lasting moat or a feature gap competitors will close."

PROCESS

How the CDP landscape was mapped

01

STEP 01

CDP Market Scoping

Defined the CDP category — distinguishing between composable CDPs, packaged CDPs, and data warehouse-native approaches. Identified key competitors including Segment (Twilio), mParticle, Rudderstack, Adobe Real-Time CDP, Bloomreach, and newer entrants targeting similar SMB and mid-market buyers.

02

STEP 02

Feature & Positioning Audit

Audited each competitor across: data collection and unification capabilities, identity resolution, audience segmentation, integration breadth (ad platforms, CRMs, analytics tools), technical skill required, pricing model (seat-based vs. event-based), and ideal customer profile (ICP). Mapped each to the buyer journey.

03

STEP 03

ICP & Segment Analysis

Analysed which buyer segments CustomerLabs wins in vs. where it loses to competitors. Focused on the no-code and marketer-first angle — where CustomerLabs' positioning as an "engineering-free CDP" resonates most strongly and where buyers prioritise raw technical capability instead.

04

STEP 04

Positioning Recommendations

Synthesised findings into a positioning map and strategic recommendations — where CustomerLabs should double down, which segments to pursue, and which competitive narratives to own. Dashboard format allows interactive exploration of the full competitive data.
