OVERVIEW

Starting from zero, literally

At 19, while still at university, I co-founded AV Solutions — a small web development agency serving local businesses in Tamil Nadu. No venture capital, no team, just a laptop, a phone, and clients who needed their first websites.

Over 18 months, we grew from a one-person side project to a small team handling end-to-end digital presence for retail and service businesses — web design, SEO, and digital strategy included.

THE REAL LESSON

"Running AV Solutions didn't teach me how to code better — it taught me how to talk to customers, translate their vague goals into concrete deliverables, and own the outcome when things don't go to plan."

LESSONS

What founding a company taught me

The hard parts had nothing to do with technology. Scoping client expectations, pricing work fairly, collecting payments, managing revision requests without scope creep — these were the skills that turned technical ability into a business.

I wrote every proposal, handled every client call, and made every hiring decision. That ownership gave me a PM's empathy for engineering constraints, a founder's bias for speed, and a consultant's ability to turn complexity into clarity.
