OVERVIEW

A roadmap is a prioritisation argument, not a release calendar

Most roadmaps are timelines with feature names. This one is different — it's a living document that captures the reasoning behind every prioritisation decision, connecting user problems to product bets to measurable milestones. The goal is to show not just what gets built, but why it gets built in that order.

The roadmap covers the full product lifecycle from discovery to delivery — including problem statements, user research inputs, feature hypotheses, success metrics, effort estimates, and dependency mapping. It's structured for both strategic alignment conversations with stakeholders and sprint planning with engineering.

THE PRINCIPLE

"A good roadmap answers 'why this, why now, and what will we learn from it.' A list of features with dates doesn't. The difference is the prioritisation framework and the courage to say no."

STRUCTURE

How the roadmap is organised

01

LAYER 01

Discovery & Problem Definition

Each roadmap item begins with a problem statement — who has this problem, how often, and what's the cost of not solving it. User research inputs, interview findings, and supporting data are directly linked so any stakeholder can trace a feature back to its user evidence.

02

LAYER 02

Prioritisation Framework

Features are scored using a structured prioritisation framework — weighing user impact, strategic alignment, technical effort, and time sensitivity. The scoring is transparent and auditable so teams can challenge assumptions rather than just accepting the output. Trade-offs are documented explicitly.

03

LAYER 03

Milestone Planning

Prioritised features are mapped to milestones — not arbitrary sprints but meaningful product moments: MVP, public beta, v1.0, monetisation launch, etc. Dependencies are mapped so engineering and design teams know what has to ship before what.

04

LAYER 04

Success Metrics

Every milestone has a defined success metric — what does "done" actually mean in measurable terms. Metrics are leading indicators where possible (user activation, task completion rates) rather than lagging outputs (revenue). The roadmap is a live doc: metrics are updated as data comes in.
